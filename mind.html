MIND and CONSCIOUSNESS

There are multiple definitions of consciousness (Merriam-Webster): the quality or state of being aware especially of something within oneself the state of being characterized by sensation, emotion, volition, and thought: mind: the element or complex of elements in an individual that feels, perceives, thinks, wills, and mainly reasons…

Two very different ideas/opinions about Mind/Consciousness/AI are coming from two significant mathematicians Marvin Minsky and Roger Penrose:

Marvin Minsky wrote two very important books about the Mind and the complexity of the computer systems and that mind can occur from the complexity of the systems:

  1. The society of mind (https://www.amazon.com/Society-Mind-Marvin-Minsky/dp/0671657135);
  2. The emotion machine (https://www.amazon.com/Emotion-Machine-Commonsense-Artificial-Intelligence/dp/0743276647).

WHY PEOPLE THINK COMPUTERS CAN'T (https://web.media.mit.edu/~minsky/papers/ComputersCantThink.txt)

Roger Penrose also wrote two very important books about his argument/reimplementations of Gödel's theorem (Penrose–Lucas argument) and connections with his ideas that from higher complexity and more computation Mind cannot occur:

  1. The emperor's new mind: concerning computers, minds, and the laws of physics (https://www.amazon.com/Emotion-Machine-Commonsense-Artificial-Intelligence/dp/0743276647);
  2. Shadows of the mind (https://www.amazon.com/Emperors-New-Mind-Concerning-Computers/dp/0192861980).

Of course, there are many other very important scientists who have given their ideas and answers to some of the questions that are represented in the work of Minsky and Penrose:

  1. Scott Aaronson - “Can computers become conscious?”: My reply to Roger Penrose (https://scottaaronson.blog/?p=2756);
  2. Drew McDermott - "Penrose is Wrong" (https://www.calculemus.org/MathUniversalis/NS/10/09mcdermott.html);
  3. Selmer Bringsjord & Hong Xiao - "A refutation of Penrose's Gödelian case against artificial intelligence" (https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/09528130050111455);
  4. Andrew Ross - "Roads to Reality: Penrose and Wolfram Compared Contenders" (https://www.researchgate.net/publication/233584394_Roads_to_Reality_Penrose_and_Wolfram_Compared_Contenders)
